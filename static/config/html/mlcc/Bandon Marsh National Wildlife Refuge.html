Protected Area: Bandon Marsh National Wildlife Refuge
Designation: National Wildlife Refuge
Acres: 1,010.50
Gap Status: 2
Description: Established in 1983, Bandon Marsh Refuge is located along the picturesque southern Oregon coast near the mouth of the Coquille River and the city of Bandon. Its invertebrate-laden mudflats fuel the migration of tens of thousands of shorebirds every spring and fall, making it an essential stopover site. Young salmon and trout find sanctuary in the estuary's steep-banked tidal channels and anchored driftwood. There are two units to the Bandon Marsh NWR, totaling 889 acres: Bandon Marsh Unit and Ni-les'tun Unit.
