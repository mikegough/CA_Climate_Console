Protected Area: Franz Lake National Wildlife Refuge
Designation: National Wildlife Refuge
Acres: 726.9
Gap Status: 2
Description: Franz Lake Refuge is the largest and most intact wapato, spikerush and bulrush marsh remaining on the lower Columbia River and is known for its seasonal abundance of tundra swans. These and other migratory birds can be seen from an observation platform in the Columbia River Gorge National Scenic Area off of Washington State Route 14.
