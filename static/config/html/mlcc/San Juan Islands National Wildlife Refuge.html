Protected Area: San Juan Islands National Wildlife Refuge
Designation: National Wildlife Refuge
Acres: 455.8
Gap Status: 2
Description: The rocks, reefs and islands of the San Juan Islands National Wildlife Refuge were set aside in 1960 to provide important habitat for migratory birds and other wildlife. Black oystercatchers, Brandt s cormorants, rhinoceros auklets, and pigeon guillemots are among the bird species that nest on Refuge islands. Harbor and elephant seals regularly use the shorelines to rest, molt and give birth to pups. Additionally, the untrammeled islands are home several rare plants including brittle prickly-pear cactus, bear s foot sanicle, and California buttercup.
