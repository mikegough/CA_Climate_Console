Protected Area: Grays Lake National Wildlife Refuge
Designation: National Wildlife Refuge
Acres: 33,741.1
Gap Status: 2
Description: Grays Lake NWR is managed by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (Service) to protect a portion of the historic Grays Lake marsh and provide resting and feeding habitat for migrating waterfowl. Grays Lake NWR is located in Southeast Idaho, 30 miles north of Soda Springs, Idaho. The Refuge lies within Grays Lake, a high elevation 22,000 acre bulrush marsh.
Link: https://www.fws.gov/refuge/grays_lake/
