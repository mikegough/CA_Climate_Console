Protected Area: Pathfinder National Wildlife Refuge
Designation: National Wildlife Refuge
Acres: 16,850.8
Gap Status: 2
Description: Pathfinder National Wildlife Refuge is comprised of 16,806 acres and was established in 1909. Today the refuge consists of four separated land parcels called Sweetwater Arm, Goose Bay, DeWeese Creek, and Sage Creek. The Refuge's boundaries have been modified several times since it's establishment. In 1936 Executive Order 7425 established the present Refuge ""as a refuge and breeding ground for birds and other wildlife"". Its major habitats of open water wetlands, uplands consisting of shrub and grasslands and alkali flats support a diversity of wildlife.
