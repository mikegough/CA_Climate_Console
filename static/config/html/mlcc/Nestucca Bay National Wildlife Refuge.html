Protected Area: Nestucca Bay National Wildlife Refuge
Designation: National Wildlife Refuge
Acres: 3,760.4
Gap Status: 2
Description: Established in 1991 to protect and enhance habitat for Dusky Canada Geese (a species of concern) and Aleutian Cackling Geese (formerly endangered), Nestucca Bay Refuge actively engages in pasture management with local dairy farmers to offer prime habitat for geese during the winter. In fact, the verdant pastures around Nestucca Bay harbor six subspecies of these so-called ""white-cheeked geese"", including the world's population of Semidi Islands Aleutian Cackling Geese.
