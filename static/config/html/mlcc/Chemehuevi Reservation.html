Protected Area: Chemehuevi Reservation
Designation: Tribal Lands
Acres: 30,815.9
Gap Status: Unknown
Description: As part of the Great Basin Culture Area, the Chemehuevi a branch of the Southern Paiute, have been persistent occupants of the Mojave Desert. Known to themselves as Nuwu, (The People) they have been nomadic residents of the Mojave Desert s mountains and canyons and the Colorado River shoreline for thousands of years.
