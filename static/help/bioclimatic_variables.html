The Climate Departure (Bioclimatic Variables) Chart shows the distribution of cell values within the selected area for a set of bioclimatic variables for the historical period (1971-2000) as well as future time periods (2016-2045 and 2046-2075).

Climate departure refers to the patterns seen in the charts. Where climate departure is high, there will be a large gap between the future and historical curves. Where climate departure is low, there will be more overlap between the future and historical curves.

The reporting unit illustrated in figure 2 would have a high climate departure because there is no overlap between the historical curves and the future curves. Where this is the case, the future climate in the selected reporting unit is expected to deviate significantly from the historical climate conditions.

Figure 2: High Climate Departure

The reporting unit illustrated in figure 3 would have a low climate departure because there is a significant amount of overlap between the historical curves and the future curves. Where this is the case, the historical climate conditions within the reporting unit are expected to exist in the future (though not necessarily in the same locations within the reporting unit).

Figure 3: Low Climate Departure
